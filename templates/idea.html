Project Overview: Sepsis Survival Predictor

What is Sepsis?

Sepsis is a potentially life-threatening condition that occurs when the body's response to an infection causes injury to its own tissues and organs. It can progress rapidly and lead to septic shock, multi-organ failure, and death if not treated promptly.

How Sepsis Attacks the Body

Sepsis usually starts with an infection (such as pneumonia, urinary tract infection, or skin infection). The immune system releases chemicals into the bloodstream to fight the infection, but instead of helping, these chemicals trigger widespread inflammation. This can lead to blood clots, leaky blood vessels, and reduced blood flow to vital organs, causing organ damage.

Symptoms of Sepsis

  • Fever, chills, or feeling very cold
  • Rapid heartbeat or breathing
  • Confusion or disorientation
  • Extreme pain or discomfort
  • Low blood pressure

Precautions and Prevention

  • Stay up-to-date with vaccinations
  • Practice good hygiene (handwashing, wound care)
  • Seek medical help for infections promptly
  • Be vigilant about changes in health, especially in vulnerable populations (elderly, immunocompromised)

How We Predict Sepsis Survival

Our machine learning model uses three key inputs:

  • Age: The age of the patient
  • Gender: 0 for Male, 1 for Female
  • Episode Number: Represents the number of sepsis episodes or hospitalizations

Based on these inputs, the model predicts the likelihood of survival: either Alive or Dead.
